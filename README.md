# Uber Ride Analytics (SQL + Power BI)

## Project Overview

This project analyzes 150,000 Uber ride booking records using MySQL and Power BI to uncover operational, financial, and customer behavior insights.

The project follows an end-to-end analytics workflow:

CSV Dataset → MySQL Data Cleaning → SQL Analysis → Power BI Dashboard → Business Insights

## Key Findings

### 1. Booking Performance

Out of 150,000 total bookings:

* Completed Rides: 93,000
* Completion Rate: 62%
* Unsuccessful Bookings: 57,000
* Unsuccessful Booking Rate: 38%

The largest unsuccessful booking category was driver-side cancellations.

---

### 2. Revenue Performance

For completed rides:

* Total Revenue: ₹47.26 Million
* Average Booking Value: ₹508.18
* Highest Booking Value: ₹4,277
* Lowest Booking Value: ₹50

---

### 3. Vehicle Revenue Analysis

Revenue generated by each vehicle category:

* Auto: ₹11.73 Million (23,155 completed rides)
* Go Mini: ₹9.41 Million (18,549 completed rides)
* Go Sedan: ₹8.54 Million (16,676 completed rides)
* Bike: ₹7.14 Million (14,034 completed rides)
* Premier Sedan: ₹5.73 Million (11,252 completed rides)
* eBike: ₹3.30 Million (6,551 completed rides)
* Uber XL: ₹1.41 Million (2,783 completed rides)

Average fares across vehicle categories remained remarkably similar:

* Go Sedan: ₹512.03
* Premier Sedan: ₹509.57
* Bike: ₹509.11
* Go Mini: ₹507.38
* Auto: ₹506.48
* Uber XL: ₹505.30
* eBike: ₹503.46

### Key Insight

Although Auto generated the highest revenue, the analysis revealed that revenue differences were driven primarily by booking volume rather than fare variation.

---

### 4. Driver Cancellation Analysis

Driver cancellations accounted for:

* Driver Cancellations: 27,000
* Driver Cancellation Rate: 18%

Top driver cancellation reasons:

* Customer Related Issue: 6,837
* Customer Was Coughing/Sick: 6,751
* Personal & Car Related Issues: 6,726
* More Than Permitted People: 6,686

### Key Insight

Driver-side cancellations were broadly distributed across multiple reasons rather than being concentrated in a single operational issue.

---

### 5. Payment Method Analysis

Revenue contribution by payment method:

* UPI: ₹21.27 Million (41,834 rides)
* Cash: ₹11.76 Million (23,114 rides)
* Uber Wallet: ₹5.67 Million (11,206 rides)
* Credit Card: ₹4.75 Million (9,320 rides)
* Debit Card: ₹3.81 Million (7,526 rides)

### Key Insight

UPI was the dominant payment method, generating the highest revenue and demonstrating strong customer preference for digital payments.

### Key Insight

Revenue distribution remained balanced throughout the year, indicating stable booking demand.

## Business Recommendations

### 1. Reduce Driver-Side Cancellations

Driver cancellations accounted for 27,000 bookings, making them the largest contributor to unsuccessful rides.

Recommended actions:

* Monitor cancellation trends by reason.
* Identify high-cancellation locations and time periods.
* Improve driver engagement and ride acceptance rates.
* Investigate recurring operational issues.

Even a 10% reduction in driver cancellations could potentially recover approximately 2,700 additional bookings.

---

### 2. Improve Ride Completion Rate

Only 62% of bookings were successfully completed.

Recommended actions:

* Track completion rate as a primary KPI.
* Reduce driver cancellations and no-driver-found cases.
* Monitor completion trends by vehicle type and location.

Improving completion rates directly impacts both revenue and customer satisfaction.

---

### 3. Leverage Auto as a High-Volume Revenue Segment

Auto generated the highest revenue because it completed the highest number of rides rather than charging higher fares.

Recommended actions:

* Maintain strong Auto availability in high-demand locations.
* Prioritize Auto driver allocation during peak periods.
* Monitor volume trends for fleet planning decisions.

---

### 4. Strengthen Digital Payment Adoption

UPI generated ₹21.27 Million in revenue and was the most preferred payment method.

Recommended actions:

* Promote UPI-based offers and incentives.
* Encourage repeat usage through loyalty campaigns.
* Track payment trends for customer retention strategies.

---

### 5. Use Dashboard-Based Monitoring

The Power BI dashboard enables monitoring of:

* Bookings
* Completion Rates
* Revenue
* Vehicle Performance
* Payment Trends
* Customer Ratings

Recommended actions:

* Monitor KPI performance regularly.
* Review booking status trends.
* Analyze revenue by vehicle category.
* Use interactive filters to identify operational patterns.

## Project Outcome

This project demonstrates how SQL and Power BI can be used together to transform raw ride-booking data into actionable business insights.

Key findings revealed that:

* Driver cancellations were the largest contributor to unsuccessful bookings.
* UPI dominated customer payment preferences.
* Customer satisfaction remained consistently high across vehicle categories.
* Revenue performance was influenced more by booking volume than fare levels.

A major takeaway from this analysis was that operational efficiency and ride fulfillment have a greater impact on business performance than pricing differences between vehicle categories.
